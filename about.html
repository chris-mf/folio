Design ethos.

01

Be aware of well-being outcomes

Whether you intentionally design for one or not someones well-being will be impacted by the work you produce.

Itâ€™s important to be conscious of long behaviours we are enabling through the products and services we design.

02

Have a learning mindset

There will always be new things to learn and tools to use. You will never be an expert in all of them all of the time. Focus on always learning rather than trying to become a master of everything.

03

Understand the problem

The best thing that you can do for yourself and for the project is realise that you can't solve it in a silo.

By calling in subject matter experts and coupling it with challenging assumptions throughout the process, you can solve it collectively.

04

Have an action bias

Making it real as quickly as possible is the only way to get a shared understanding of the problem and to build the confidence required to progress.

You will get real reactions, not baseless opinions.
