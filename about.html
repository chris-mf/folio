Design ethos

01

Everyone is responsible for the well-being outcome

Whether you intentionally design for one or not someones well-being will be impacted by the work you produce. Everyone on the team, both the client side and practitioners are responsible for the well-being outcome of the project. This isnâ€™t just how it looks or interacts, but what is the long behaviours we are informing by the products we release to the world.

02

Have a learning mindset

There will always be new things to learn and tools to use. You will never be an expert in all of them all of the time. Focus on always learning rather than trying to be a master. This will give you permission to fail which is one of the best ways to learn.

03

Focus on understanding the problem not offering a solution

The best thing that you can do for yourself and for the project is realise that you can't solve it on your own. You have to call in subject matter experts and people who know more about that topic. Couple this with challenging assumptions throughout the process. Only then you can you aim to solve it collectively.

04

Everyone can mentor

05

Have an action bias

Making it real as quickly as possible is the only way to get a shared understanding of the problem and to build confidence that will enable you to progress throughout the process. You will get real reactions, not baseless opinions.
